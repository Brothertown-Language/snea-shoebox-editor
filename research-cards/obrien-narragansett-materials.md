# Research Card: Frank Waobu O'Brien (Moondancer) — Narragansett Pedagogical Materials

## 1. Source Description

**Author:** Dr. Frank Waobu O'Brien (Moondancer) — Narragansett language teacher and revitalization activist. Co-founder of the Aquidneck Indian Council with Julianne Jennings (Strong Woman). Has taught Narragansett language classes for decades.

## 2. Dialect Affiliation

Narragansett (Eastern Algonquian, Y-dialect) — based primarily on Roger Williams' *A Key into the Language of America* (1643), supplemented by comparative Algonquian data.

## 3. Key Works Detail

### Guide to Historical Spellings and Sounds (2004/2005)
- **Charts A, B, C** — the most accessible reference for interpreting Williams' orthography
- **Chart A:** Massachusett vowel ambiguity (based on Goddard & Bragdon 1988)
- **Chart B:** Narragansett spelling-to-sound mapping (Williams' orthography → phonemes)
- **Chart C:** Master table of New England Algonquian spelling-to-sound conventions
- **Canonical URL:** bigorrin.org/waabu11.htm (intermittent availability)
- **ERIC PDF:** files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED490469.pdf (always available)

### Grammatical Studies in the Narragansett Language (2009)
- Comprehensive grammatical sketch of Narragansett
- Covers phonology, morphology, syntax
- Based on Williams' data with comparative Algonquian analysis
- 2nd edition (2009) available via ERIC

### Indian Grammar Dictionary for N-Dialect (2000)
- Pedagogical dictionary organized for learners
- "N-Dialect" refers to the Massachusett/Narragansett group (vs. L-dialect Delaware, Y-dialect Mohegan)
- Practical teaching tool rather than scholarly reference

## 4. Orthographic System

O'Brien uses a pedagogical transcription system that:
- Maps Williams' orthographic conventions to probable phonemic values
- Uses standard Roman letters with diacritics for vowel quality/length
- Provides practical pronunciation guides for learners
- Is less technical than a full IPA transcription but more accessible

## 7. Key Features

- **Charts A, B, C** are the most accessible reference for colonial orthography interpretation
- O'Brien's work bridges **17th-century sources and modern learners**
- The **Grammatical Studies** provides the only modern grammatical description of Narragansett
- The **N-Dialect** framing is useful for understanding the Massachusett-Narragansett relationship

## 8. Research Gaps

- O'Brien's pedagogical system has not been systematically compared with academic Algonquianist analyses (Aubin, Goddard)
- The relationship between O'Brien's transcriptions and the WLRP modern orthography needs study
- The Scribd materials are behind a login wall, limiting access
