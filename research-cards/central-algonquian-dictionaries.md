# Central Algonquian Dictionaries and Vocabularies

## Language Family Group
Central Algonquian (areal grouping, not genetic)

## Resources

### Cree-Innu-Naskapi Group

#### Eastern James Bay Cree Dictionary
- **Language:** Cree (East Cree, Northern/Southern dialects) [crl/crj]
- **Type:** Online dictionary
- **Access:** https://dictionary.eastcree.org
- **Orthography:** Cree Roman orthography + Syllabics
- **Notes:** English-Cree, French-Cree, Cree-English. Northern and Southern dialects.

#### itwêwina Plains Cree Dictionary
- **Language:** Cree (Plains Y-dialect) [crk]
- **Type:** Online dictionary
- **Access:** https://itwewina.altlab.app
- **Orthography:** Standard Roman Orthography (SRO) for Plains Cree
- **Notes:** Searchable by short or very long Cree words (polysynthetic). Developed by Algonquianist linguists.

#### Online Cree Dictionary (Maskwacis)
- **Language:** Cree (Plains Cree, Woods Cree) [crk/cwd]
- **Type:** Online dictionary
- **Access:** https://www.creedictionary.com
- **Orthography:** SRO + Syllabics
- **Notes:** Aggregates 4 dictionaries: Alberta Elders' Cree Dictionary, Earle Waugh, Arok Wolvengrey, Maskwacis.

#### A Dictionary of the Cree Language (Watkins 1865/1938)
- **Language:** Cree [crk]
- **Type:** PDF
- **Access:** https://www.creeliteracy.org
- **Notes:** Historical dictionary, useful for diachronic comparison.

### Ojibwe-Potawatomi Group

#### Ojibwe People's Dictionary
- **Language:** Ojibwe (Southwestern Ojibwe) [ojb]
- **Type:** Online talking dictionary
- **Size:** 7,000+ entries with audio
- **Access:** https://ojibwe.lib.umn.edu
- **Orthography:** Double-vowel Ojibwe orthography
- **Notes:** Includes cultural items, photographs, historical documents. Voices of Ojibwe speakers.

#### Nishnaabemwin Online Dictionary
- **Language:** Ojibwe (Odawa dialect) [otw]
- **Type:** Online dictionary
- **Size:** 12,000+ words
- **Access:** https://dictionary.nishnaabemwin.atlas-ling.ca
- **Orthography:** Nishnaabemwin practical orthography
- **Notes:** Odawa dialects of Lake Huron, especially Manitoulin Island. Part of the Algonquian Dictionaries Project.

#### A Dictionary of the Ojibway Language (Baraga 1853/1992)
- **Language:** Ojibwe [oji]
- **Type:** Book/PDF
- **Access:** https://archive.org
- **Notes:** Historical dictionary by Bishop Frederic Baraga. Important for diachronic comparison.

#### Potawatomi Language Dictionary
- **Language:** Potawatomi [pot]
- **Type:** Online dictionary
- **Access:** https://www.potawatomidictionary.com
- **Orthography:** Practical Potawatomi orthography
- **Notes:** Provided by the Citizen Potawatomi Nation. Based on elder speakers.

### Sauk-Fox-Kickapoo Group

#### A Concise Dictionary of the Sauk Language
- **Language:** Sauk [sac]
- **Type:** PDF
- **Access:** https://www.sacandfoxnation-nsn.gov
- **Orthography:** Sac & Fox Nation authorized phonetic system (1995)
- **Notes:** Based on fieldwork 1990s–2000s. Sauk is closely related to Meskwaki (Fox) and Kickapoo.

#### Kickapoo Vocabulary (Voorhis 1988)
- **Language:** Kickapoo [kic]
- **Type:** Book (Algonquian and Iroquoian Linguistics, Memoir 6)
- **Size:** 205 pp
- **Access:** University of Manitoba
- **Notes:** Based on fieldwork 1964–1970. Part of the Fox-Sauk-Kickapoo dialect continuum.

### Miami-Illinois Group

#### Myaamia ILDA Dictionary
- **Language:** Miami-Illinois [mia]
- **Type:** Online dictionary + mobile app
- **Access:** https://aacimotaatiiyankwi.org
- **Orthography:** Myaamia practical orthography
- **Notes:** Myaamia Center, Miami University. Also available as printed Miami-Peoria Dictionary.

### Other Central

#### The Menomini Language (Bloomfield 1962)
- **Language:** Menominee [mez]
- **Type:** Book/PDF
- **Size:** 515 pp
- **Access:** https://archive.org
- **Notes:** Bloomfield's definitive grammatical description with lexicon. Menominee is one of the most conservative Algonquian languages phonologically.

#### English-Shawnee Dictionary (Mason Emerson)
- **Language:** Shawnee [sjw]
- **Type:** PDF
- **Access:** Scribd
- **Notes:** Practical dictionary.

## Relevance to SNEA

Central Algonquian languages are the most conservative phonologically and provide the best evidence for PA reflex verification. Cree preserves PA vowel length and cluster distinctions that are collapsed in SNEA. Ojibwe provides extensive comparative data for the Y-dialect/N-dialect classification. Menominee (Bloomfield) is the most phonologically conservative Algonquian language and is essential for verifying PA reconstructions used in SNEA calibration.

## Key Phonological Features for Cross-Source Calibration

- Cree preserves PA *e: and *i: distinction (collapsed in SNEA colonial sources)
- Cree preserves PA cluster distinctions (*hp *ht *hk *šk) — directly relevant to SNEA preaspiration problem
- Ojibwe provides the Y-dialect reference for PA *r → /y/ (vs. SNEA N-dialect /n/)
- Menominee preserves PA vowel length most faithfully of any Algonquian language
- Menominee preserves PA *h preaspiration — critical for SNEA preaspiration inference
- Sauk-Fox-Kickapoo preserve PA *θ and *l distinctions collapsed in SNEA
- Miami-Illinois provides transitional evidence between Central and Eastern Algonquian
