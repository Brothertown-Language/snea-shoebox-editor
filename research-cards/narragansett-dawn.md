# Research Card: The Narragansett Dawn (1935–1936) — Tribal Language Lessons

## 1. Source Description

**Title:** *The Narragansett Dawn*

**Editors:** Princess Red Wing (Mary Cong Morgan, 1896–1987) and Ernest Hazard — both Narragansett tribal members.

**Publication:** Monthly newspaper, May 1935 to September 1936. 17 issues total.

**Content:** History, culture, and language of the Narragansett tribe. Includes 14 language lessons teaching Narragansett vocabulary and phrases.

**Full text:** Available via Dawnland Voices (dawnlandvoices.org) and queer.archive.work (full PDF).

## 2. Dialect Affiliation

Narragansett (Eastern Algonquian, Y-dialect) — the language of the Narragansett people of Rhode Island. The language lessons are based primarily on Roger Williams' *A Key into the Language of America* (1643), supplemented by other sources.

## 3. Significance

The Narragansett Dawn is significant because it represents the **only known published attempt at language teaching by tribal members themselves** in the early 20th century. It is a tribal community language work, not a colonial or academic production.

## 4. Estimated Entry Count

14 language lessons, each containing vocabulary and phrases. The total lexical content is modest but represents a curated selection of words deemed important by the tribal community.

## 5. Orthographic System

The language lessons use a simplified spelling system based on Williams' orthography, adapted for 20th-century readers. The orthography is not systematic in a linguistic sense but reflects the editors' understanding of the language.
