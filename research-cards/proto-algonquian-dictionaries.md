# Proto-Algonquian Dictionaries

## Language Family Group
Proto-Algonquian (reconstructed ancestor of all Algonquian languages)

## Resources

### Hewson (1993) — Computer-Generated Dictionary of Proto-Algonquian

- **Type:** Online dictionary (originally book, 1993)
- **Size:** 4,066 entries with English index
- **Access:** https://protoalgonquian.atlas-ling.ca
- **Method:** Computer-generated from comparative data; typos corrected 2015–2017
- **Orthography:** Standard PA transcription (Bloomfieldian)

### Aubin (1975) — A Proto-Algonquian Dictionary

- **Type:** Book (Mercury Series, Canadian Ethnology Service Paper No. 39)
- **Size:** ~2,300 reconstructions
- **Access:** Sciarium, Perlego, Project MUSE
- **Method:** Traditional comparative method
- **Orthography:** Standard PA transcription

## Relevance to SNEA

Both dictionaries provide the Proto-Algonquian reconstructions used throughout the SNEA project for comparative calibration. Hewson is the larger and more recent corpus; Aubin is cited extensively in the Narragansett research card (#1362) and provides the PA forms used to disambiguate Williams' vowel mappings.

## Key Phonological Features

- PA vowel system: *a *a: *e *e: *i *i: *o *o:
- PA consonant system: *p *t *k *m *n *s *š *h *w *j *θ *l *r
- PA clusters: *hp *ht *hk *hč *šp *št *šk *xp *xt *xk
- PA *θ → /n/ in N-dialects (Wampanoag, Narragansett), /l/ in L-dialects, /r/ in R-dialects, /y/ in Y-dialects (Mohegan)
