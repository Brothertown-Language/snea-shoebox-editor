# Research Card: Thomas Mayhew Jr. — Martha's Vineyard Wampanoag Missionary Work (1640s–1650s)

## 1. Source Description

**Author:** Thomas Mayhew Jr. (1621–1657) — First English missionary to the Native Americans of New England. Son of Governor Thomas Mayhew of Martha's Vineyard. Learned the Wampanoag language and began preaching in Wampanoag in the 1640s.

**Date:** 1640s–1650s.

**Location:** Martha's Vineyard (Noepe).

**Significance:** Mayhew was the **first English person to learn Wampanoag fluently** and preach in the language. His work predates John Eliot's Massachusett mission by nearly a decade. He established the mission that his nephew Experience Mayhew would continue into the 18th century.

## 2. Dialect Affiliation

Martha's Vineyard Wampanoag — the same dialect later documented by John Cotton Jr. (1666–1678) and Experience Mayhew (1727).

## 3. Surviving Materials

**No systematic vocabulary by Mayhew Jr. is known to survive.** His linguistic work is documented through:
- References in his father's correspondence
- Descriptions in later sources (e.g., Experience Mayhew's *Indian Converts*)
- The fact that he preached in Wampanoag and translated religious materials

He died at sea in 1657 (shipwreck), which may explain the lack of surviving manuscripts.
