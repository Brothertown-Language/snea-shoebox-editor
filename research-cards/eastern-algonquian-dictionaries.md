# Eastern Algonquian Dictionaries and Vocabularies

## Language Family Group
Eastern Algonquian (true genetic subgroup within Algonquian)

## Resources

### Mi'gmaq/Mi'kmaq Online Talking Dictionary

- **Language:** Mi'kmaq [mic]
- **Type:** Online talking dictionary with audio
- **Size:** 6,000+ words, each recorded by 3+ speakers
- **Access:** https://mikmaqonline.org
- **Orthography:** Mi'kmaq Roman orthography (Smith-Francis, Pacifique)
- **Notes:** Each headword used in an accompanying phrase. Multiple speakers allow hearing dialectal variation.

### Passamaquoddy-Maliseet Dictionary (Francis & Leavitt)

- **Language:** Maliseet-Passamaquoddy [pqm]
- **Type:** Online portal + book (2nd edition)
- **Size:** 18,000+ words
- **Access:** https://pmportal.org (verified reachable)
- **Orthography:** Practical Passamaquoddy-Maliseet orthography
- **Notes:** 50+ years of collaboration among speakers, educators, and linguists. Includes video archive of conversations.

### Western Abenaki Dictionary (Day 1994)

- **Language:** Western Abenaki [abe]
- **Type:** Book (Mercury Series, Canadian Ethnology Service Paper 128), 2 volumes
- **Size:** Vol. 1: Abenaki-English, 538 pp
- **Access:** https://archive.org/details/rosettaproject_abe_ortho-1
- **Orthography:** Practical Western Abenaki orthography
- **Notes:** Documents the language as spoken in the last half of the 20th century. Speakers from Odanak, Quebec and Missisquoi Bay region.

### Nulhegan Abenaki Dictionary (2025)

- **Language:** Western Abenaki [abe]
- **Type:** Online dictionary
- **Access:** https://abenakionline.com
- **Notes:** Released March 2025 with National Archives grant. First publicly shareable, freely accessible English-Abenaki dictionary.

### The Lenape Talking Dictionary

## Relevance to SNEA

Eastern Algonquian is the closest genetic relative of the SNEA languages. Mi'kmaq and Maliseet-Passamaquoddy preserve PA clusters and vowel distinctions that are collapsed in SNEA colonial sources. Lenape (Delaware) is transitional between SNEA and Central Algonquian and provides crucial evidence for the Mahican analysis (#1369). Abenaki is the closest living relative of the SNEA languages and provides the best comparative data for resolving SNEA phonological questions.

## Key Phonological Features for Cross-Source Calibration

- Mi'kmaq preserves PA *l (lost in SNEA) — useful for PA correspondence verification
- Maliseet-Passamaquoddy preserves PA cluster distinctions collapsed in SNEA
- Lenape preserves PA *r (→ /l/ in Unami, /n/ in Munsee) — key for dialect classification
- Abenaki preserves PA *h preaspiration patterns — directly relevant to the SNEA preaspiration problem
- All Eastern languages have systematic orthographies (unlike SNEA colonial sources) — provide clean comparative data
