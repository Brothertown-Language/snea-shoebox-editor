# Research Card: A Modern Mohegan Dictionary — Fielding & Costa (2006)

## 1. Source Description

**Title:** *A Modern Mohegan Dictionary*

**Authors:** Stephanie Fielding (compiler, Mohegan Tribe Council of Elders) and David J. Costa (linguistic editor). Prepared for the Council of Elders of the Mohegan Tribe of Connecticut.

**Date:** 2006 edition.

**Publisher:** Mohegan Tribe, Council of Elders, Cultural and Community Programs Department.

**Full text:** Available on Archive.org and the Brothertown Indians website.

## 2. Dialect Affiliation

Mohegan-Pequot (Eastern Algonquian, Y-dialect) — the language of the Mohegan Tribe of Connecticut. Closely related to Pequot (the two are often considered dialects of a single language). The dictionary draws primarily on the diaries of Fidelia Fielding (the last fluent speaker, d. 1908) and other historical sources.

## 3. Reconstruction Methodology

David Costa, a leading Algonquianist linguist (also known for his work on Miami-Peoria and Myaamia language revitalization), developed the Mohegan alphabet and reconstructed the phonology:

1. **Primary sources:** Fidelia Fielding's diaries (c. 1900), Prince-Speck (1904), Ezra Stiles (1762), and other historical documentation
2. **Comparative Algonquian:** Uses Proto-Algonquian reconstructions to resolve orthographic ambiguities
3. **Costa alphabet:** A practical phonemic orthography developed specifically for Mohegan
4. **Database compilation:** The dictionary draws from an accumulated database edited by Costa, with entries marked for source (FF = Fidelia Fielding, etc.)

## 4. Orthographic System (Costa Alphabet)

The Costa alphabet is a phonemic orthography for Mohegan:

- Vowels: a, e, i, o, u (with length marked)
- Consonants: p, t, k, č, s, š, h, m, n, w, y
- Preaspiration: written as h before stops (hp, ht, hk)
- The alphabet is designed to be practical for language learners while remaining phonemically accurate

## 5. Dictionary Structure

- **Mohegan-to-English section:** Organized alphabetically by Mohegan headword. Each entry includes part of speech, English gloss, inflected forms, and example sentences.
- **English-to-Mohegan glossary:** Reverse lookup section.
- **Grammar section:** Precedes the dictionary, providing essential grammatical information.

## 8. Key Features

- **Only modern Mohegan dictionary** — the definitive lexical resource for the language
- **Professional linguistic editing** — Costa is a leading Algonquianist
- **Source-attributed entries** — each entry marked with its historical source (FF, PS, etc.)
- **Example sentences** — many entries include sample sentences from Fielding's diaries
- **Grammar section** — provides morphological context for the vocabulary
- **Cross-dialectal value** — enables Mohegan vs. Wampanoag comparison for the shoebox editor

## 9. Utility as Comparison Baseline

This dictionary is critical for the shoebox editor project because:

1. Mohegan-Pequot is the **closest sister language** to Wampanoag/Massachusett within the SNEA family
2. The phonemic orthography provides a **reconstructed pronunciation** for comparison
3. The **source-attributed entries** allow tracing each word back to its historical attestation
4. It enables **cross-dialectal comparison** — words that differ between Mohegan and Wampanoag can reveal sound changes and dialectal innovations

## 10. Research Gaps

- The relationship between the Costa alphabet and the historical orthographies (Prince-Speck, Fielding, Stiles) is not fully documented in a single reference
- Systematic comparison of Mohegan dictionary entries with Wampanoag cognates has not been done for the shoebox editor project
- The dictionary's coverage of specialized vs. basic vocabulary needs assessment for cross-source comparison
