# Research Card: James Noyes — Pequot Vocabulary (1690)

## 1. Source Description

**Author:** Rev. James Noyes II (1640–1719) — Harvard graduate (1659), pastor of the church in Stonington, Connecticut (1674–1719). One of the founders of Yale College.

**Date:** 1690.

**Location:** Groton, Connecticut.

**Content:** A Pequot vocabulary list compiled by Noyes during his ministry in southeastern Connecticut.

**Status:** The existence of this vocabulary is documented in secondary sources (NY Times 1998, LiquiSearch), but the current location and accessibility of the original manuscript are uncertain.

## 2. Dialect Affiliation

Pequot (Mohegan-Pequot, Eastern Algonquian, Y-dialect) — the language of the Pequot people of southeastern Connecticut. Noyes served in Stonington, which was Eastern Pequot territory.

## 3. Significance

If the manuscript survives, this would be the **earliest known Pequot vocabulary** (1690), predating Ezra Stiles (1762) by 72 years and Prince-Speck (1904) by 214 years. It would provide crucial data on the Pequot language in the 17th century, before the period of intense language decline.
